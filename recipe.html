World's Tastiest Breakfast Sandwich!


Years before your regular brunch spot started serving its version, the breakfast sandwich was a utilitarian, on-the-go meal for factory workers in 19th century London. On their way to the daily grind, downtrodden workers would speedily chug a cup of coffee at a street vendor's stand, then grab a breakfast sandwich to eat as they made their way to the factory. They were originally called "bap" sandwiches, named for the soft rolls used to hold the egg and meat filling. Sometimes bacon or sausage were the main meat component, but a simple slathering of sausage grease wasn't uncommon.

As the industrial revolution came into full swing in the USA, the breakfast sandwich made its way across the Atlantic. We even got our own version, stuffed with eggs, ham, green pepper, and onion, known as the Denver sandwich. This part of the story is where boy meets girl, or in this case, where Ryan met his favourite recipe: The World's Tastiest Breakfast Sandwich!

Ingredients

Below is a list of ingredients needed for two Breakfast Sandwiches

  • Whole Wheat English Muffin (x2)
  • 1 Leaf of Romain Lettuce
  • Handful of Banana Peppers
  • 4 Slices of Cheddar Cheese
  • 2 Organic Brown Eggs
  • 1/2 Cup of Onion
  • Ketchup
  • 4 Slices of Bacon



Cooking Directions

Two teflon (non-stick) large cooking pans, a toaster, cutting board, bread knife, cheese knife, tongs, plastic flipper (for eggs) and paper towel are required to cook these epic breakfast sandwiches!

  1. Cut 4 cheddar cheese slices equally (enough to cover each side of the english muffin)
  2. Slice the half cup of onion (Click here to watch an instructional video)
  3. Cut the English Muffins in half
  4. Place both cooking pans on elements and begin heating ONE of the pans to Med-High, 4/6, or 6/8. (Depending on the scale your oven uses)
  5. Place all 4 pieces of bacon on the pan that is heated up
  6. Cook bacon to your liking (fatty, medium or crispy)
  7. Once cooked, place bacon on two sheets of paper towel to absorb grease
  8. Turn second pan on to begin heating to Med-High, 4/6, or 6/8. (Depending on the scale your oven uses)
  9. Put sliced onion into pan where bacon was cooked (optional to leave bacon grease in pan) cook for 2 minutes while continuously flipping and stirring
  10. Place both English Muffins into toaster, cook until golden brown, level 5/6 on most toasters
  11. Once the second pan is up to temperature, crack both eggs onto pan (Don't break yoke if you like them overeasy!) and cook to your liking
  12. When English Muffins are toasted, place on plate, and put sliced cheese on all four sides
  13. Take onions off pan, turn element off, and place half of onions on two button sides of the english muffins
  14. Place two pieces of bacon ontop of onions
  15. Evenly distribute banana peppers ontop of bacon slices
  16. Place cooked egg ontop of each english muffin that has banana peppers
  17. Add two squirts of ketchup ontop of the eggs
  18. Evenly break the leaf of romain lettuce into two equal parts and place ontop of ketchup
  19. Take the top half of the english muffin (smaller half), and place on top bottom half
  20. Lastly, find a comfortable place to sit down, take a deep breathe and FEAST!
